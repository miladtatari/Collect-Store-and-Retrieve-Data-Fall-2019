5)Read ?facet_wrap. What does nrow do? What does ncol do? What other options control the layout of the individual panels? Why doesnt facet_grid() have nrow and ncol argument? (10 points)

?facet_wrap

nrow or ncolcontrols the plot layout by indicating the number of rows or columns. They are for facet_wrap() only. By default, column number is the number that fits the page and then however the rows are needed. Other option controls are: Scales (fix or free), shrink (can fit scales to raw data or statistics), labeller (takes one data frame and returnes a list of vectors), as.table (facets are laid out like a table or plot), switch (changing the positions of the labels), drop (hiding or showing factor levels), dir (determining horizontal or vertical direction), strip.position (placing the labels on either of the four sides). nrow or ncol are not defined for facet_grid as there is no control on number of rows and columns for facet_grid.
